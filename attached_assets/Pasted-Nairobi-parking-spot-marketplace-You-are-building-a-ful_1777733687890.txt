Nairobi parking spot marketplace
You are building a full production-grade peer-to-peer parking marketplace for Nairobi. Read the entire brief, think deeply, expand the vision with your sharpest ideas, then present approximately 20 substantial phased plan before any code. Wait for "next."
The pain:
Parking in Westlands, CBD, Upperhill, Kilimani, and Hurlingham is a daily crisis for tens of thousands of Nairobi commuters. NCC parking is unreliable, unsafe, and often full. Mall parking is limited and increasingly monetised. Meanwhile, hundreds of private driveways, church compounds, office building basements, residential compounds, and unused plots sit completely empty between 7am and 6pm on weekdays. The owners of these spaces earn nothing. The commuters circling the block for 40 minutes burning fuel and arriving late to meetings would happily pay Ksh 300–500 to guarantee a spot. Nobody has connected these two groups with a clean, trustworthy product.
What you are building:
A two-sided marketplace. Space owners list their parking spot with photos, availability hours, price, and access instructions. Commuters search by destination, see available spots on a map, book and pay via Mpesa, and receive access instructions (gate code, contact number, what to say at the gate). The platform takes 15% commission. No hardware required. No barriers. Pure software connecting two parties who already exist and already want this.
Critical nuances:
Access instructions are sensitive — the platform must only reveal them after confirmed payment. Cancellation policy needs to be clear and enforced — a spot owner who cancels repeatedly should be penalised. A commuter who books and doesn't show should forfeit a portion. Ratings on both sides are essential — a poorly rated spot (bad access, owner not responsive) should be surfaced less. The map interface is the core of the product and must be genuinely good — not a pins-on-Google-Maps afterthought. Monthly subscription parking (a commuter reserves the same spot every weekday for a month) is a huge upsell — predictable income for the owner, zero daily friction for the commuter. Corporate accounts — companies that want to secure 10 spots near their Upperhill office for visiting clients — represent high-value B2B revenue.
Expand this. Think about: a "waiting list" feature for high-demand zones; integration with Google Maps for turn-by-turn to the exact spot; a spot owner payout dashboard; surge pricing on event days (e.g. Ngong Racecourse events, Kasarani concert days); a "safe parking" badge for spots with CCTV; partnerships with churches whose compounds are empty on weekdays; and anything else that makes this the go-to parking layer for Nairobi.
Hosting: Next.js on Vercel with edge functions for real-time spot availability. Backend and database on Railway. Mpesa Daraja API integration server-side. Map features via Mapbox or Google Maps API. Docker-ready for VPS.
